# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters, founders, product leaders, and technical teammates who are evaluating Anjelika Tan for product, platform, operations, or technical product roles. They are likely scanning quickly first, then reading deeper if the portfolio signals clear judgment, practical ownership, and comfort with messy systems.

## Product Purpose

This portfolio exists to make Anjelika's product and platform experience legible, memorable, and credible. It should show that she can turn messy product systems into clear workflows, reusable logic, and operationally useful tools. Success means a visitor quickly understands what she is strong at, sees concrete proof in the case studies, and feels confident enough to contact her or review the resume.

## Brand Personality

Clear, capable, grounded, lightly playful. The voice should feel thoughtful and specific, not grand or self-mythologizing. Personality can come through in small interactions, confident copy, and visual rhythm, but the work should stay the center.

## Anti-references

Avoid over-dramatic self-branding, vague transformation language, and motivational-poster phrasing. Avoid generic corporate resume polish that sounds impressive but says little. Avoid forced whimsy, excessive easter eggs, or visual tricks that distract from the product systems work.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with clarity under pressure: the first screen should make the portfolio's promise obvious without inflated language.
2. Show systems, not slogans: case studies should make product logic, integrations, permissions, workflows, and operational tradeoffs visible.
3. Keep the human signal: use warmth, restraint, and small playful touches so the site feels personal without becoming cute.
4. Make depth scannable: recruiters should get the point fast, while deeper readers can follow the system evolution.
5. Treat motion as explanation: animation should reveal relationships, transitions, and progress rather than decorate every section.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast, semantic HTML, keyboard-accessible interactions, readable line lengths, and responsive layouts across mobile and desktop. Provide reduced-motion alternatives for all animation and avoid relying on color alone to communicate meaning.
